GoodJira: Next Generation Project Management for AI Agents

June 18, 2025
AI Productivity

The need to track software projects has existed ever since humans started instructing machines.

The Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) is a lot more than the code - it's about the people, the processes, the feedback, the upgrades, the incidents. The code is just the beginning. As software begins interacting with the real world (users) it has a life of it's own. If your software becomes a critical part of your customer's business processes, the stakes become infinitely higher than a vibe-coded webapp.

As more coding tasks (across the stack) begin delegated away to SWE-Agents, I as their product manager have a greater need to track and validate their work. The user experience for managing your small army of SWE-agents is lacking. Some efforts to manage this chaos are task lists - some tools build this into the experience while others work well with creating markdown files detailing tasks and asking SWE-agents to update the task-list as it accomplishes each unit of work. There are also Jira MCP integrations that in theory should allow me to track and enforce some processes on these SWE-agents.

Personally at my day-job I wouldn't be considered a process-heavy PM. I build enough trust with my engineers that I trust them to deliver on our shared vision. However, I can't say the same about SWE-agents. I've found the need to be very structured in my instructions and processes when building using these agents. For example, "write a test based on the specification, and then implement the feature and make sure tests pass". SWE-agents feel magical, but (today) they still need a little babysitting.

Tracking work during development is one thing, but once your software is out there, you will have an ever-growing backlog of improvements and bugs. Your users will always want more and your software will always have bugs (Halting problem). Therefore I need a tool to organize my backlog and prepared in a way that can easily be sent to SWE-agents to start implementing.

Then there is the incident management and operational aspects of software systems. I desire a system where a software incident occurs at 3am, it's triaged by SRE-agents to bring the system back to functional. The root cause is determined by an SWE-Agent that has a deep understanding of your stack and code-base. Tasks to fix the bug are broken down and tracked appropriately for SWE-agents to make a PR. By the time I wake up around 5:30am I see that an incident occured last night, systems were operational in 5m and a new build with the bug fix was promoted to production by 3:30am.

Why is tracking all this imporant? Ultimately we're serving customers with our software. If our software is not meeting their needs in any way (functionality, reliability, performance) I would like to know. Then I'd like to have the tools necessary to prioritize and tackle the improvements and bugs that best deliver customer value.

Does Jira or similar tools (Linear, Trello etc.) meet the needs of software project management in the era of SWE-Agents? Maybe it's time for GoodJira.
